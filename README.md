# 📊 HR Attrition & Employee Satisfaction Analysis

## 🏢 Business Context

Employee attrition impacts organizational performance through increased hiring costs, productivity loss, and reduced workforce stability.  

This project analyzes HR data to identify the key factors driving employee turnover and provides data-driven recommendations to support strategic retention planning.

### Objectives

- Identify high-risk employee segments  
- Understand key drivers of attrition  
- Analyze the impact of satisfaction, compensation, and career growth  
- Provide actionable HR recommendations  

---

## 📁 Dataset Overview

The dataset contains employee-level workforce data including:

- **Demographics:** Age, Gender, Marital Status  
- **Job Information:** Department, Job Role, Business Travel  
- **Compensation:** Monthly Income, Income Bands  
- **Career Growth Metrics:** Years at Company, Years Since Last Promotion, Promotion Gap  
- **Satisfaction Indicators:** Job Satisfaction, Work-Life Balance, Environment Satisfaction  
- **Target Variable:** Attrition (Yes/No)  

---

## 📌 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

- Overall Attrition Rate: Calculated from dataset  
- Highest Risk Segment: Early-tenure employees (0–2 years)  
- Major Attrition Drivers Identified:
  - Overtime workload  
  - Promotion stagnation  
  - Lower income bands  
  - Low job satisfaction  

---

## 🔍 Exploratory Analysis Highlights

### 1️⃣ Tenure Impact

Employees within their first 0–2 years show the highest attrition rates, suggesting potential onboarding or early engagement challenges.

### 2️⃣ Overtime Influence

Employees working overtime demonstrate significantly higher attrition compared to those with balanced workloads. This indicates workload management as a critical retention factor.

### 3️⃣ Compensation Sensitivity

Lower income bands exhibit elevated attrition levels, highlighting the influence of compensation structures on retention.

### 4️⃣ Career Progression Gaps

Employees with longer promotion gaps show increased likelihood of leaving, suggesting career stagnation concerns.

### 5️⃣ Satisfaction Correlation

Lower job satisfaction and work-life balance scores strongly correlate with higher attrition rates.

Attrition patterns are concentrated within specific workforce segments rather than being randomly distributed.

---

## 🎯 High-Risk Employee Profile

Employees most at risk typically exhibit:

- 0–2 years tenure  
- Active overtime workload  
- Lower income band  
- Low job satisfaction  
- Extended promotion gap  

This segmentation enables targeted and proactive retention strategies.

---

## 💡 Strategic HR Recommendations

### Immediate Actions
- Monitor and regulate overtime in high-risk departments  
- Strengthen early-tenure engagement and onboarding programs  
- Introduce structured promotion review cycles  

### Medium-Term Focus
- Review compensation strategy for lower income tiers  
- Enhance career progression transparency  

### Long-Term Strategy
- Implement continuous employee satisfaction tracking  
- Develop workforce monitoring dashboards for proactive attrition management
